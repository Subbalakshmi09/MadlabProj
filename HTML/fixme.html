All About Capybaras

Introduction

The capybara (Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris) is the largest rodent in the world. Native to South America, these semi-aquatic mammals are known for their friendly and social nature.

Interesting Facts

  • Capybaras can grow up to 134 cm (4.4 ft) in length and weigh up to 66 kg (146 lbs).
  • They are excellent swimmers and can hold their breath underwater for up to five minutes.
  • They live in groups, often with 10-20 individuals, but sometimes up to 100.
  • Capybaras are herbivores and mainly eat grasses and aquatic plants.

Capybaras in the Wild

Capybaras are found in wetlands, rainforests, and grasslands near bodies of water in South America. They have a close relationship with many other animals and are often seen with birds, monkeys, and even predators who tolerate their presence.
